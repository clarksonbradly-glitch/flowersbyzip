FlowersByZip was built by a former local florist who saw firsthand how wire services extract value from local shops — and the customers who love them. This is our attempt to fix that, one ZIP code at a time.

The insider secret every florist knows

When you order flowers through a company like order intermediaries, you probably assume most of your money goes to the local florist who makes your arrangement. It doesn't.

Wire services charge shops monthly membership fees just to receive orders. Then they take a percentage of every order. Then there are "delivery fulfillment" charges, "technology fees," and required minimum order purchases from their own supplies.

This isn't a criticism of wire services — they serve a real purpose. It's just math. More margin means more motivation to make something exceptional. FlowersByZip drives the orders that florists love most.

Direct order vs. intermediary order — same $75

Customer pays $75.00
Wire service order fee (~20%) – $15.00
Monthly membership (prorated) – $8.00
Fulfillment / tech fees – $4.50
Required supply purchases – $5.00
Florist actually receives $42.50
That $42.50 must cover flowers, labor, packaging, and delivery — leaving many florists near or below cost.

The origin story

Built by a florist, for florists.

If you're a local florist, you already know this story — because it's probably yours too.

We were a local flower shop. And like so many local florists, we depended heavily on wire service providers because, at the time, that's where the orders came from. It felt like a lifeline. What we didn't fully understand until it was too late was that those wire services weren't giving us enough money to actually sustain the business.

By the time you paid your lease, your utilities, the cost of goods, your staff, and every other expense that comes with running a shop — the margins on wire service orders were simply not enough to survive on. What a florist truly needs to stay in business are direct orders. Orders where the full amount stays with the shop. Orders where the customer's dollar actually reaches the hands of the person arranging their flowers.

Like too many local florists across this country, we were eventually forced to close our doors. The craft was there. The passion was there. The community loved us. But the economics, driven in large part by a dependency on wire service revenue, made it impossible to keep going.

FlowersByZip exists because we don't want that to happen to anyone else.

Zip says: Did you know? On a $75 order through a wire service provider, the florist might only receive $42.50 after all the fees are deducted. That's why ordering direct matters. 🦉
We didn't close because people stopped loving flowers. We closed because the system that was supposed to help us was quietly making it impossible to survive.
— FlowersByZip Founder

This directory is our way of fighting back — not with anger, but with a simple tool that connects customers directly to the florists in their community. No middlemen. No fees taken off the top. Just a real order going to a real local shop.

If we can help even one florist stay open who might otherwise have closed, this was worth building.
